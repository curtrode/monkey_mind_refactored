Banff Centre for Arts and Creativity  

Dear Adjudication Committee,

I am writing in support of my application to the Computational Writing Residency at Banff Centre. I am a poet and teacher working across print and digital forms, and I direct the Digital Culture & Data Analytics major and minor at Texas Christian University. My electronic literature work has appeared in *The New River* and Jim Andrews' *Vispo ~ Langu(im)age: Interactive, Visual, and Sound Poetry*, and is archived or cited in the NEXT, ELMCIP, and the Electronic Literature Directory.

My project, *Monkey Mind*, stages a transition from stochastic text generation into LLM-driven improvisation. What exists now is a two-voice stochastic duet and a single-voice improvisation prototype. The piece is not autobiographical, but it does dramatize patterns of relational anxiety and overthinking that I have experienced and observed in others.

The core question for me is both technical and poetic: can a computational text move from combinatory mechanics into responsive dialogue without collapsing into cliche? I am especially interested in whether prompt design can introduce what I call lyric suspicion, where the language begins to question its own assumptions rather than simply "express" them. My authorship is in vocabulary design, formal constraint, prompt direction, and editorial curation of outputs.

I returned to this project after several years away and, almost immediately, encountered the Banff call. That timing clarified the next step. LLMs let me work more ambitiously, but they do not replace the need for serious artistic community and critique. I want to test this work in conversation with practitioners whose influence already shapes it, especially Nick Montfort, and to broaden that conversation through Banff's faculty and cohort.

With the duet and single-voice prototype already built, during the September 7-18, 2026 residency I will build and test a multi-model version of *Monkey Mind*, then refine it into a presentation-ready demonstration excerpt and a documented prompt framework for continued development after Banff. The results will feed directly into course design and creative projects I am planning for next year, while also supporting publication, performance, and installation possibilities.

Sincerely,  
Curt Rode
